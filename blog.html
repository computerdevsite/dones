Understanding Viruses, Malware, Spam, and Antivirus

What is a Virus?

A virus is a type of malicious software designed to spread from one computer to another, often causing harm. These harmful programs can corrupt files, slow down your system, or steal personal information. Viruses are usually spread through email attachments, infected websites, and file-sharing.

To protect yourself from viruses, ensure you have updated antivirus software and avoid downloading files from untrusted sources.

What is Malware?

Malware (malicious software) is a broad term that includes any type of software intended to harm or exploit any device, service, or network. It includes viruses, ransomware, spyware, and other harmful programs.

Malware can compromise your data, track your online activity, or cause systems to crash. A good defense against malware includes keeping your operating system and software updated, using strong passwords, and enabling firewalls.

What is Spam?

Spam refers to unsolicited and often irrelevant or inappropriate messages sent over the internet, particularly via email. Spam is mostly used for advertising purposes, and sometimes it contains malicious links or attachments.

What is Antivirus Software?

Antivirus software is a program designed to detect and remove viruses and other malicious software from your computer. It scans files, monitors for suspicious activity, and blocks threats in real time.

Using a reliable antivirus solution is essential for protecting your data and privacy. Keep the antivirus software updated and run regular system scans to identify potential threats.
